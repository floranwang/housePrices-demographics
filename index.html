House Price and Demographic Trends 2008-2016

Created by: Chenchen Pan and Flora Wang


Here we explore how house values (as measured by the Zillow Home Value Index) and demographics have changed between 2008 and 2016.

Approach:

  1. Show the overall trends of percent change in racial groups and house values on the county and state level across the U.S.

  2. Define and calculate an index as

    3. Index-pop = Change in Population ⁄ Change in House Value

    Index-race = % Change in House Value ⁄ % Change in Race

Overall trends for 2008-2016 (1263 counties)

To the right are two maps, showing the absolute average house value change and the absolute population change for each county.

To better understand and quantify the relationship between the change in house value and the change in demographics, we calculated and visualized the ratio of this relationship in the index analysis below.

It's hard to observe the correlation between demographic change and house value change on the county level, but there are some interesting findings on the state level.

State Level Trends

Overall, the population change and house value change are positively correlated, except 6 states (AZ, WI, GA, OH, VT, CT ).
